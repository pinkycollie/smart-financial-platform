Why Focus on Deaf Clients?

The deaf community represents an underserved market with unique financial needs. By providing accessible services, you can grow your practice while making a meaningful difference.

Growing Market

There are approximately 48 million Americans with hearing loss and over 1 million who are culturally Deaf. Yet fewer than 20% report having a financial advisor they trust.

Less Competition

Few financial professionals have the tools or resources to effectively serve deaf clients. Those who do gain a significant competitive advantage.

Client Loyalty

Deaf clients who find accessible financial services tend to be highly loyal and refer others, building a stable client base through word-of-mouth.

Financial Accessibility Gap

Studies show that deaf Americans face significant barriers to financial services, resulting in financial outcomes that lag behind their hearing peers:

  • 78% of deaf adults report difficulty understanding financial concepts due to language barriers
  • 63% of deaf Americans feel uncomfortable or frustrated when meeting with financial professionals
  • 55% lower retirement savings on average compared to hearing counterparts with similar income
  • 91% of deaf clients say they would prefer a financial advisor who provides ASL-accessible services
  • 4.8x more likely to refer friends and family to a financial professional who offers deaf-accessible services
